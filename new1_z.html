COLLAGE

A collage is a work of art, from the French word "coller" meaning "to glue," that is created by assembling and gluing different materials such as paper, photographs, and fabric onto a flat surface to form a new, unified whole. This technique can refer to the process of cutting, arranging, and sticking these items, or to the final artwork itself. Collages can also be created digitally by combining electronic images. Key aspects of collage Materials: A wide variety of materials can be used, including magazine cutouts, personal photos, different types of paper, fabric, and even found objects. Technique: The fundamental technique involves sticking various pieces of material onto a background. Different cutting methods, such as using scissors, X-acto knives, or even ripping edges, can be employed. Purpose: Collages serve as a visual medium for expressing ideas, analyzing issues, and developing concepts. They can also be used to create a visual narrative or a mood board of images. Digital Collages: In the digital age, collages can also be created by combining digital images on a computer or digital platform, such as Adobe Express. Application: The technique has been used in various forms of art and design, from traditional mixed-media art to digital design and even creating mood boards or visual presentations.
